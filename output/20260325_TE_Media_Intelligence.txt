## **5. PRIVATE CREDIT UNTER MASSIVEM DRUCK – REDEMPTION-CRISIS MATERIALIZES**

**Szenarien heute (25. März) in den Newströmen abgebildet:**
- Two of the biggest names in private credit, Ares Management Corp. and Apollo Global Management Inc., blocked investors from getting even half of the money they wanted out of their funds, a sign of mounting strain in the $1.8 trillion market. The $10.7 billion Ares Strategic Income Fund limited withdrawals to 5% of shares after clients sought to redeem 11.6%, according to a letter to shareholders Tuesday.
- That followed the $15.1 billion business development company, Apollo Debt Solutions, which said Monday it was imposing the same cap after requests to pull 11.2%.
- Morgan Stanley's North Haven Private Income Fund received redemption requests totaling nearly 11% of its outstanding shares; it honored less than half of them.

**Marktinterpretation:**
- DoubleLine Capital CEO Jeffrey Gundlach said markets are stuck in a holding pattern with few assets delivering meaningful returns, as he warned that stresses emerging in private credit could deepen if investors rush for liquidity.
- "We all know that the private credit industry was deluged with redemption requests ; it far exceeded the 5%."

**Systematisches Risiko:**
- the IMF has explicitly warned that the risks from non-bank financial institutions, if they materialize, may spill over into banks. Jeffrey Gundlach of DoubleLine Capital has been blunter, calling private credit the top candidate to start the next financial crisis.

**Warum relevant:** Private Credit ist **$1,8 Billionen groß**, viele Banken haben direkte Exposure. Eine Cascade von Redemptions könnte zu Asset Fire-Sales, Mark-to-Market-Verlusten und Spread-Blowout in Fixed Income führen. Druck auf Emerging Market Credit, CLOs, und alle illiquiden Assets.
